___History___

The Indian Premier League (IPL) was started by the Board of Control for Cricket in India (BCCI) in 2008 as a professional Twenty20 cricket league in India. The idea for the IPL was first proposed by the then BCCI vice-president Lalit Modi, who had the vision to create a league that would bring together the best domestic and international cricket players to compete in a fast-paced and entertaining format of the game.
Modi presented his proposal to the BCCI, which was initially met with some resistance from traditionalists who were opposed to the idea of franchise-based cricket. However, Modi was able to convince the BCCI of the commercial potential of the league, and the first season of the IPL was held in 2008 with eight teams representing various cities across India.
The teams were owned by a combination of Indian and international investors, and players were bought through an auction system. The league was an instant success, with millions of cricket fans tuning in to watch the matches live or online.
Over the years, the IPL has grown in popularity and has become one of the most-watched sports leagues in the world, with a global audience of millions of fans. The league has also attracted top international players from around the world, making it a truly global competition

Orange Cap
The Orange Cap is an award presented to the leading run-scorer in the Indian Premier League (IPL) during a particular season. The player who scores the most runs throughout the tournament wears the Orange Cap while on the field. The Orange Cap was introduced in IPL 2008, and it has been a part of the league ever since. At the end of each match, the Orange Cap is passed on to the player who is leading the run-scoring charts. At the end of the tournament, the player with the most runs is awarded the Orange Cap as a recognition of his outstanding performance.

Purple Cap
The Purple Cap is an award presented to the leading wicket-taker in the Indian Premier League (IPL) during a particular season. The player who takes the most wickets throughout the tournament wears the Purple Cap while on the field. The Purple Cap was also introduced in IPL 2008, along with the Orange Cap, and has been a part of the league ever since. Similar to the Orange Cap, at the end of each match, the Purple Cap is passed on to the player who is leading the wicket-taking charts. At the end of the tournament, the player with the most wickets is awarded the Purple Cap as a recognition of his outstanding performance. In case of a tie, the player with a better economy rate is given the Purple Cap.
